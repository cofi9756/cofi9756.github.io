Beyond the Classroom

Interests & Activities

The various hobbies and strange things I participate in outside school. Each one has contributed to my growth in ways that complement my professional path.

Interest Photo 1

Music? I played guitar and flute for some time

Throughout highscool and a little bit towards the end of elementary school I played the guitar. Once in highscool I picked up the flute for a required band class. I have found that the practice and learning techniques I gained from both of these musical instruments are applicable to both school and work in a variety of ways.

Interest Photo 2

Cooking

Cooking has been surprisingly valuable as cooking forces you to follow a recipe, otherwise end up with sad mush, helping me to undestand and read instructions carefully. It also occasionally tests creative problem solving when an ingredient is missing or process does not go quite right, but a creative solution or substitution can still save it. Mise en place also taught me to be prepared for things you know are coming, otherwise you have to make a mad scramble to get ready which could potentially leave other important tasks by the wayside.

Interest Photo 2

Hiking

Hiking is less learning skills for the workforce and is more about learning how to relax and unwind every so often. Listen to the birds and the various animals along the forst path. Laugh at a racoon or squirrel as it does something stupid off in the corner. Admire various flowers as the other plants poke and stab you. In other words, admire the things worth admiring and ignore the other stuff because that other stuff is not important. Though, sometimes the pokey plants are also pretty.

Interest Photo 3

Gaming

I know that gaming is generally laughed at in a professional setting as unusuable, but I have found several benefits. Firstly, it very effectivley tests hand eye coordination and how quickly you can get these two systems to cooperate. Secondly, it consistently tests critical think and problem solving with the various puzzles thrown at players. Finally, it tests cooperation and communication through multiplayer cooperative games or uncooperative games.
